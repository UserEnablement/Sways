Scenario 1
Oliver — Cross-pod conversation

You're in a cross-team meeting. A colleague from the Oliver Admin pod asks: "Isn't WEBS just Oliver Admin with a different skin? They both do group benefits, right?" You need to correct this — clearly, without making them feel wrong for asking.

What is the most accurate response?

The rule to carry: Always ask whether a system is an Oliver product or a client platform Oliver supports. WEBS is the clearest example of the second model. Oliver Admin is the clearest example of the first. They are not the same thing configured differently — they represent two different commercial and operational relationships.
Scenario 2
GH Pod — Support ticket

You're supporting the GH pod and receive this message from a GroupHEALTH client services team member: "One of our plan administrators is panicking — they submitted a class change for an employee this morning and now the record is locked. They think they broke the system. I can't get the record to move. What's happening?"

What is the correct diagnosis and response?

Key term — Pending / Locked: A record is locked while a submitted change awaits GroupHEALTH review. This is expected workflow, not an error. Records lock to protect data integrity during review. Changes are typically approved within 24 hours. The most common mistake is treating a locked record as a system failure and escalating unnecessarily — which creates unnecessary work and erodes plan administrator trust.
Scenario 3
Sales / Scoping — New prospect conversation

A sales colleague is preparing for a call with a prospect. The prospect manages group benefits for their employees and runs a pension plan and hour-bank arrangement for union members. They say they want "something like the system GroupHEALTH uses." Your colleague asks you: "Can I just point them at WEBS?"

What should your colleague know before that call?

Two rules in one scenario: (1) WEBS is not for sale — it belongs to GroupHEALTH. When a prospect wants group benefits administration, the Oliver conversation is Oliver Admin or Oliver MultiPlan, not WEBS. (2) Pension + hour-bank = MultiPlan, not Admin or WEBS. WEBS handles group benefits only; it does not cover pension plans, hour banks, or union trust fund environments. Those belong in Oliver MultiPlan — a separate platform with a different architecture and client base.
