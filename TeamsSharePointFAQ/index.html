About the Change

Answer

Oliver has moved to a pod-based operating model — where each pod has a clear focus, a defined team, and specific clients and products it serves. Our current tools — Teams channels, SharePoint, and Slack — were built for a different way of working and no longer reflect how we are actually organised. Aligning our tools to our operating model now means every pod has a clear, consistent home for their work, conversations, and documents. The longer we wait, the harder the gap becomes to bridge.

Answer

Three things are changing. First, our Microsoft Teams channels are being restructured to match the pod model — each pod gets a dedicated channel with a consistent set of subchannels. Second, Slack is being retired — all communication will move into Teams as our single platform. Third, SharePoint will be rebuilt to reflect the same pod structure, though this will happen after the Teams transition and on a separate timeline.

Answer

Yes. The pod model defines how Oliver is organised and how work flows. This change ensures our tools reflect that — so where you work in Teams and where you find documents in SharePoint matches the pod you belong to and the work you do.

Answer

This is a leadership decision, sponsored by Riyaad. The initiative is being led by Nicole (Teams restructure), Alex (SharePoint rebuild), and Agnes (change management and communications). It has been designed to support the pod operating model for the long term.

Teams

Answer

Each pod will have a dedicated Teams channel: Oliver Admin, Oliver Multi, Oliver Connect, Oliver GH, Oliver TPA, and Oliver Studio. Each channel follows a consistent subchannel structure — General, Support Queue, Product Release Notes, and client-specific subchannels where relevant. Oliver Basecamp will serve as the shared space for company-wide announcements, general discussion, team updates, and events.

Answer

The new structure is designed so that people work within their pod's channel. Access to channels outside your pod will depend on your role and the governance model being established. More detail on access and permissions will be shared before the new structure goes live.

Slack

Answer

Having conversations split across Slack and Teams means that context gets lost, things get missed, and there is no single place to find what you need. Consolidating into Teams — our primary collaboration platform — means everything lives in one place, aligned to your pod. It also removes the overhead of managing two separate communication tools.

Answer

We will provide clear guidance on how to handle Slack content before the platform is retired. Any conversations or files that need to be preserved will have a clear migration path. Nothing will be switched off without advance notice and a full transition plan in place.

A specific date has not yet been confirmed. You will be given clear advance notice — and full guidance on the transition — before Slack is retired. We will not switch it off until you are ready.

Using the New Structure

Answer

Use the General channel for day-to-day conversations, questions, daily updates, product-wide announcements, and anything that applies across your whole pod. Use the client subchannels for anything specific to a particular client — support issues, project updates, escalations, and client-specific conversations. Always tag the relevant person or team when posting so the right people see your message. If it applies to a client, it belongs in that client's channel — not General.

Answer

For now, daily status updates move to the General channel of your pod. This keeps them visible to the right people and in one place. Longer term, the daily check-out practice itself is being reviewed — the goal is to move toward more meaningful productivity signals rather than manual daily posts. More will be shared on this as it develops.
