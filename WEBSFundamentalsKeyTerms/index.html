GroupHEALTH's self-service platform for accessing and managing group benefits. GroupHEALTH owns and uses it. Oliver supports, maintains, and evolves it.
~80% of the GH pod's support work is related to this platform.

Definition 2
The employer-side user responsible for managing employee benefits in WEBS — typically the HR lead or office manager. GroupHEALTH holds them responsible for keeping records current and on time.
This is who used to call GroupHEALTH for every question. Now they go directly to WEBS.

Definition 3
WEBS's digital onboarding tool. Sends new employees an email invitation to complete their own benefits application online — replacing paper enrolment cards. Also a selling platform for optional benefits such as Optional CI and Member Advantage.
No paper form. No manual data entry by HR.

Definition 4
A record is locked while a submitted change awaits GroupHEALTH review. This is expected workflow — not an error. Changes are typically approved within 24 hours. The lock protects data integrity during review.
The most common WEBS misconception: plan administrators often think something has gone wrong.

Definition 5
A client billing unit in WEBS. Plan administrators managing benefits for multiple groups switch between these to view, update and download statements for each group separately.
One employer may have several of these — one per plan design or policy.

Definition 6
The employer-side section of WEBS recording each employee's class, salary, occupation, and employment date. This is the administrative record of who the employee is and what their job details are.
Distinct from their coverage details — that's a different record type.

Definition 7
The employee-side section of WEBS recording personal information, dependents, beneficiary designations, and coverage details. This is what the member sees as their own benefits record.
Works alongside the EE Profile — together they form the full member picture in WEBS.
Drop term here
Definition 8
Coordination of Benefits. The process of recording when a member is covered under more than one insurance plan, so claims are directed to the correct carrier first. Prevents double-payment and ensures correct sequencing.
Often confused with CBO (Core Benefits Override) — they sound similar but are different processes.
Drop term here
Definition 9
Experience Reporting System. A reporting tool within WEBS that generates detailed claims and benefits experience reports per group and per ledger, on demand. Used heavily by GroupHEALTH client services teams.
Generates reports like claims paid vs claims incurred — the data behind how a plan is performing.
Drop term here
Definition 10
Billing System Administration. The section of WEBS showing an employee's coverage amounts for each eligible benefit. This is the financial layer — how much coverage a member has for each benefit type.
Added to WEBS key terms based on SME review — important for billing accuracy checks.
