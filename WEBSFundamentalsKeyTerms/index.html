A record is locked while a submitted change awaits GroupHEALTH review. This is expected workflow — not an error. Changes are typically approved within 24 hours. The lock protects data integrity during review.
The most common WEBS misconception: plan administrators often think something has gone wrong.

Definition 6
The employer-side section of WEBS recording each employee's class, salary, occupation, and employment date. The administrative record of who the employee is and what their job details are.
Distinct from their coverage details — that's a different record type.
Drop term here
Definition 7
The employee-side section of WEBS recording personal information, dependents, beneficiary designations, and coverage details. What the member sees as their own benefits record.
Works alongside the EE Profile — together they form the full member picture in WEBS.
Drop term here
Definition 8
Coordination of Benefits. The process of recording when a member is covered under more than one insurance plan, so claims are directed to the correct carrier first and double-payment is prevented.
Don't confuse with CBO (Core Benefits Override) — similar acronym, different function entirely.
Drop term here
Definition 9
Experience Reporting System. A reporting tool within WEBS that generates detailed claims and benefits experience reports per group and per ledger, on demand. Used daily by GroupHEALTH client services teams.
Generates the data that shows how well a plan is performing over time.
Drop term here
Definition 10
Billing System Administration. The section of WEBS that shows an employee's coverage amounts for each eligible benefit — the financial layer showing how much coverage a member actually has under each benefit type.
The coverage amounts view — distinct from who the member is (EE) or what their plan includes (BP).
