Here's the pattern across each scenario.

Awareness

"Who even decided this?" — When someone's questioning the decision itself, they haven't yet accepted the reason for the change. Clarifying the business case and urgency comes before anything else.

Desire

"I'll do it, but I'm not happy." — Understanding doesn't create buy-in. Desire gaps need listening and honest conversations about personal impact — not more information or pressure.

Knowledge

"I'm not sure what I'm supposed to do." — Confusion about process or expectations is a Knowledge gap. Targeted instruction, job aids, and clarity about what "good" looks like are the right tools here.

Ability

"I know what to do — it just takes me twice as long." — Knowledge and Ability are different. Ability gaps need practice time, coaching in real conditions, and removing barriers — not more training.

Reinforcement

"We did it for a while, then things slipped." — Change erodes without active follow-through. Recognition, accountability, and visible leadership consistency are what keep new behaviours from fading.

The single most important principle: Address the first missing element — not the most obvious one, not the easiest one. Work left to right through A → D → K → A → R. Fixing a later gap before an earlier one almost never works.
